# RESUME MASTERCLASS

## What do you think how much time does a recruiter spend time on your resume ?

> Only 15 to 30 seconds. Our Resume is just like a TINDER profile. If you are not able to impress the recruiter in the first 15-30 seconds, then you will be left swiped.
> On first 15-30 seconds, our resume should make a positive impact on the recruiter. It should be able to grab the attention of the recruiter and make them want to read more about you.
> As everyone has short of time. Because recruiter have to go through hundreds of resumes.

#### Myths for Software Engineers

- No Photo is required in a resume. It wastes space in the resume. We are not going for modeling role.
- No need to mention your gender or nationality if you are applying for a hybrid or remote role.
- No full address is required.
- Always have your full name, email address and phone number in your resume. Sometimes, recruiter may want to contact you for further details like notice period, current job etc.
- No need of Objective or Summary section in the resume. There is not even one reason to keep that, it just wastes a lot of space. No need to mention about I am hard working, enthusiastic, passionate etc. Every company is looking for a hard working, enthusiastic and passionate candidates.
- Every resume should be just 1 PAGE.. 1 PAGER is best. Always mention top impactful things of your career.

#### What sections then we need to mention in the resume ?

There are mainly 4 important sections we need to mention as per following priority:

At the top of the resume, we should have full name, email address and phone number. It must also contain Github and LinkedIn profile links.

1. Projects / Experience (Most important and should be amazing section)
   - Projects you are working in your current job.
   - For experienced people, you can mention your personal projects as well. For example, if you are switching from Angular to React, Python to Node.js but you got no chance to work on that technology in your current job, then you can mention your personal projects in that technology.
   - Try to always put github and live URL links (even of product if working in product based companies). These social proofs enhance the credibility of your resume.
   - Try to add metrics, numbers and mention technical details. Suppose, if you built a ecommerce website, reduced the page load time to less than 400ms. (Then what you did to achieve this - techincal keywords). After your feature, the conversion rate increased by 20%. Leading a team for 5 junior developers.. etc.
   - Always keep short bullet points for describing projects.. developed this, implemented that, reduced this, increased that etc. We can mention technical features lazyloading, sharding, infinite scrolling, pagination, caching etc. Not more than 2 lines. 1 liners are best.

2. Skills / Tech Stack Section
   - Always mention a lot of keywords in this section for ATS (Applicant Tracking System). ATS is a software that recruiters use to filter resumes based on keywords.
   - So, whatever have worked on should be put here in form of keywords.

3. Education
   - Which college, which school, which branch. Graduation, High School education etc.
   - For freshers, sometimes CGPA can be mentioned. For experienced people, it doesn't matter at all.
   - In secondary education, if you had computer science as a subject, then you can mention that as well. It will be a plus point for you.

4. Achievements / Certifications
   - Don't mention non techincal skills like cooking, bodybuilding competition something.
   - Mention technical skills like hackathon, leetcode rankings in leaderboard and all.
   - We can mention certifications like AWS, Azure, GCP, Scrum Master etc.

First section must always be first section. Rest of the sections can be rearranged. So, if you have more achievements you can prioritize it.

#### A HACK for increasing the chances of getting shortlisted

- Don't make your resume according to your skills. Make your resume according to need or job profile you are applying to.

> Suppose a backend developer => Full stack developer. Resume should be according to full stack role, not backend role. Make personal frontend projects and mention them. Similarly frontend engineer should make personal projects of backend and mention them in the resume. So, make your resume according to the job profile you are applying to.

> [!TIP]
> No one resume will work for every job profile. You have to make different resumes for different job profiles / companies. We may need to change mentioned things or challenges accordingly. We should keep different versions of our resume ready for different roles. The diff between job profile and resume will decide whether you will get shortlisted or not. Little tweaks in resume can make wonders.
